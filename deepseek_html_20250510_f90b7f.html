Key Rules of Present Perfect

1. Time Indicators

Present Perfect often uses these time expressions:

  • ever/never - for life experiences
  • already/just/yet - for recent actions
  • for/since - for durations
  • recently/lately - for indefinite recent past

I have already finished my work.

She has lived here since 2010.

2. Unfinished Time

Use Present Perfect for periods that continue to the present:

  • today, this week/month/year
  • in my life, so far, up to now

We haven't seen him this week.

So far, I have visited 10 countries.

3. Past Participle Forms

Remember these irregular forms:

Base Form Past Participle
go gone
see seen
write written
take taken

2. They _____ (just/arrive) at the airport.

3. How long _____ you _____ (know) your best friend?

5. _____ you ever _____ (eat) sushi?

Exercise 2: Rewrite in Present Perfect

1. I started learning English in 2020. (for)

2. She went to Paris last year. (already)

3. They didn't call me yesterday. (yet)

4. Do you visit London? (ever)

5. He works here. (since 2015)
